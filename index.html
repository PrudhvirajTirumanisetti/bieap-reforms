Core Examination Reforms

The 2025-26 academic year introduces significant reforms. These changes align the curriculum with national standards (NCERT), modify the examination structure, and introduce continuous evaluation to foster deeper conceptual understanding.

📚 NCERT Syllabus Transition

1st Year Science streams fully adopt the NCERT curriculum. Syllabus for other streams and 2nd Year Science is also revised.

🔢 New 5-Subject Structure

Science streams move to a 5-subject system (1 Language, 1 Elective, 3 Core Subjects), totaling 500 marks per year.

📝 Consolidated Papers

Maths (A+B) and Biology (Botany+Zoology) are now single 100-mark papers, changing the exam approach.

❓ New Question Pattern

Focus shifts to objective and analytical questions (VSAQ, SAQ, LAQ) over rote memorization, mirroring competitive exams.

📅 Internal Assessment (20%)

Mandatory continuous evaluation through tests, assignments, and projects contributes 20% to the final marks in most subjects.

🔬 Revised Marks

Theory marks for Physics & Chemistry increased from 60 to 70, keeping practicals at 30 marks.

Grading & Division System

Final results are determined by a grade point system. Students must secure at least 35% in each subject and in aggregate to pass. This chart visualizes the mark range for each grade.

Vocational OJT Evaluation

On-the-Job Training (OJT) is a mandatory 100-mark component for all vocational courses. Performance is assessed based on the following criteria by industry supervisors and college faculty.
